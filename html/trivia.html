Fun Movie Trivia

  • The Dark Knight (2008): Heath Ledger locked himself in a hotel room for 6 weeks to prepare for the role of the Joker.
  • The Matrix (1999): The iconic "green code" in The Matrix was inspired by Japanese sushi recipes.
  • Titanic (1997): The scene where Jack sketches Rose was actually drawn by director James Cameron.
  • Star Wars: A New Hope (1977): The sound of the lightsaber was created by combining the hum of an old TV and a movie projector motor.
  • Psycho (1960): Chocolate syrup was used as blood in the famous shower scene.
  • Avengers: Endgame (2019): Robert Downey Jr. hid snacks on the set and shared them during takes.
  • Harry Potter Series: J.K. Rowling revealed that the names of the houses were created on a bar napkin.
  • The Godfather (1972): The cat Marlon Brando holds in the opening scene was a stray found on set.
  • Frozen (2013): The song "Let It Go" was written in just one day.
  • Forrest Gump (1994): Tom Hanks didn’t take a salary for this movie but instead accepted a share of the profits.
  • The Lion King (1994): The "Hakuna Matata" song was almost cut from the movie.
  • The Wizard of Oz (1939): The Wicked Witch’s makeup was so toxic that the actress couldn’t eat solid food during filming.
  • Back to the Future (1985): The DeLorean was chosen because its design made it look like a spaceship.
  • Jaws (1975): The mechanical shark often malfunctioned, leading to more suspenseful off-screen moments.
  • Indiana Jones: Raiders of the Lost Ark (1981): Harrison Ford performed many of his own stunts.
  • E.T. (1982): The iconic alien voice was created by a chain-smoking woman named Pat Welsh.
  • The Avengers (2012): Mark Ruffalo wore a motion-capture suit to play the Hulk instead of relying entirely on CGI.
  • Shrek (2001): Mike Myers decided to give Shrek a Scottish accent mid-production.
  • Iron Man (2008): The script was only partially written when filming began; many scenes were improvised.
  • The Hunger Games (2012): Jennifer Lawrence accidentally kicked co-star Josh Hutcherson in the head during training.
  • The Social Network (2010): Jesse Eisenberg memorized every line of the script, including those of other characters.
  • Avatar (2009): James Cameron waited over a decade for technology to catch up to his vision.
  • The Silence of the Lambs (1991): Anthony Hopkins improvised the terrifying hissing sound during his dialogue.
  • Rocky (1976): Sylvester Stallone wrote the screenplay in just three days.
  • Goodfellas (1990): The "Funny how?" scene was improvised by Joe Pesci and Ray Liotta.
  • The Shawshank Redemption (1994): The maggots fed to the bird in the movie had to be approved by the American Humane Association.
  • Fight Club (1999): Brad Pitt had his front tooth chipped to appear more authentic as Tyler Durden.
  • It (2017): Bill Skarsgård could naturally move his eyes in different directions, adding to Pennywise’s creepiness.
  • Deadpool (2016): Ryan Reynolds paid for the writers to be on set so they could tweak the dialogue in real-time.
  • The Lego Movie (2014): Every Lego piece in the movie is designed to be buildable in real life.
  • Mad Max: Fury Road (2015): The entire movie was storyboarded before the script was written.
  • Black Panther (2018): The Wakandan language is based on Xhosa, one of South Africa's official languages.
  • Frozen II (2019): It took over 118 hours to render the movie’s most complex scene.
  • Finding Nemo (2003): Pixar's animators had to attend a marine biology class before creating the film.
  • Beauty and the Beast (1991): This was the first animated film to be nominated for Best Picture at the Oscars.
  • Aladdin (1992): Robin Williams improvised most of the Genie’s lines.
  • The Simpsons Movie (2007): Over 100 drafts of the script were written before the final version.
  • Thor: Ragnarok (2017): Over 80% of the dialogue in the movie was improvised.
  • The Godfather Part II (1974): Robert De Niro learned to speak Sicilian for the movie.
  • Interstellar (2014): The black hole visualization was so accurate it led to new scientific discoveries.
  • Wonder Woman (2017): Gal Gadot was five months pregnant during reshoots for the film.
  • John Wick (2014): Keanu Reeves trained for months to perfect his gunfight and martial arts skills.
  • The Revenant (2015): Leonardo DiCaprio ate raw bison liver for his role, even though he’s a vegetarian.
  • The Exorcist (1973): The sound of the demon’s voice was created by recording a woman smoking and drinking heavily.
  • The Great Gatsby (2013): The champagne used in the party scenes was real.
  • Jurassic Park (1993): The sound of the T-Rex’s roar was a mix of a baby elephant, a tiger, and an alligator.
  • Spider-Man (2002): Tobey Maguire actually caught all the food on the tray in the cafeteria scene—no CGI was used.
